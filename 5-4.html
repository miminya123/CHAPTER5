We can make some conclusions now.

In 5.2, we need to have \( x(t), \dot{x}(t) \) to solve \( F = m\ddot{x} = -kx \)

We can consider to use \( x(t), \dot{x}(t) \) to be the coordinates of a point in a two-dimensional space. It is called phase space.

The point can describe the state of a particle will follow a specific path in the phase plane.

All of the possible phase path constitute Phase Diagram.

In SHM, we have:\[ \begin{align*} x(t) &= A \sin(\omega t - \delta) \\ \dot{x}(t) &= A\omega \cos(\omega t - \delta) \end{align*} \]

We can use Ellipse function to rewrite: \[ \frac{x^2}{A^2} + \frac{\dot{x}^2}{A^2 \omega_0^2} = 1 \] The total energy E of the oscillator is \( E = \frac{1}{2} kA^2 \) and \( \omega_0^2 = \frac{k}{m} \), so: \[ \frac{x^2}{2E/k} + \frac{\dot{x}^2}{2E/m} = 1 \]

Phase diagram with different energy

With more total energy, the more amplitude it will get. And, the point will always be in a clockwise direction.

Damping in RLC Circuit

if we want to do experiments to show the damping in RLC Circuit

We need 3 things.

A resistor will consume energy of the circuit system. The voltage across the resistor satisfy \( V_R = IR = iR \)

A capacitor store electric charge and discharge. Capacitance is the ability to store electricity. So \( C = \frac{q}{V_C} \Rightarrow V_C = \frac{1}{C} q \) By KCL, we can get the equation that \( V_L + V_R + V_C = 0 \Rightarrow L \frac{di}{dt} + R i + \frac{1}{C} q = 0 \) And, we can have: \( \beta = \frac{R}{2L} \) and \( \omega_0 = \sqrt{\frac{1}{LC}} \) It is similar to damping string.

An inductor will create an opposite voltage ⇒Lenz’s Law. The Magnetic flux is proportional to current flowing through it, define \( L = N \frac{d\Phi}{dI} \) By Faraday’s law of electromagnetic induction, \( e = -N \frac{d\Phi}{dt} = -N \frac{d\Phi}{dI} \frac{dI}{dt} \Rightarrow V_L = L \frac{di}{dt} \)
