Oscillator

We need to talk about two-dimensional restoring forces first.

In 5.2, the restoring force is \(\vec{F} = -k \vec{x}\) .

But, in 5.3, the two-dimensional restoring force is \(\vec{F} = -k_i \vec{r}\) .

We are going to discuss it in 2 cases.

Case1.Isotropic Harmonic Oscillator

The constant is same. $$ \vec{F} = -k\vec{r} \Leftrightarrow \begin{cases} F_x = -kx \\ F_y = -ky \end{cases} $$

We can write it like 5.2 $$ \begin{equation} \left\{ \begin{aligned} \ddot{x} + \omega^2 x &= 0 \\ \ddot{y} + \omega^2 y &= 0 \end{aligned} \right. \end{equation} $$ \(\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}\) The solution would be: $$ \begin{align*} x(t) &= A\cos(\omega t - \alpha) \\ y(t) &= B\cos(\omega t - \beta) \end{align*} $$

Case2.Anisotropic Harmonic Oscillator

The constant is different. $$ \vec{F} = -k\vec{r} \Leftrightarrow \begin{cases} F_x = -kx \\ F_y = -ky \end{cases} $$

It is just like case1. $$ \begin{align*} x(t) &= A\cos(\omega_x t - \alpha) \\ y(t) &= B\cos(\omega_y t - \beta) \end{align*} $$ Where \( \omega_x = \sqrt{\frac{k_x}{m}} \text{ and } \omega_y = \sqrt{\frac{k_y}{m}}\)

Isotropic Harmonic Oscillator

We have learned how to write the equation.It is time to analysis it.To explain the path:

$$ y(t) = B\cos[\omega t - \alpha + (\alpha - \beta)] \\ = B\cos(\omega t - \alpha)\cos(\alpha - \beta) - B\sin(\omega t - \alpha)\sin(\alpha - \beta) $$

We can define \(\delta =\alpha -\beta\) and \(\cos(\omega t -\alpha)=\frac{x}{A}\)to make it easier.

The function of motion is: $$ B^2x^2 - 2ABxy\cos\delta + A^2y^2 = A^2B^2\sin^2\delta $$ It can be seen as an oval function.

Different conditions:

(1)Circle: \( \delta = \pm \frac{\pi}{2}\quad\text{and}\quad A=B \)

(2)Straight lines(Slope>0): \( \delta =0,2 \pi \) $$ B^2x^2 - 2ABxy + A^2y^2 = 0 \implies (Bx - Ay)^2 = 0 \implies y = \frac{B}{A}x $$

(3)Straight lines(Slope<0): \( \delta =\pi \ or -\pi \) $$ B^2x^2 - 2ABxy + A^2y^2 = 0 \implies (Bx - Ay)^2 = 0 \implies y = -\frac{B}{A}x $$

(4)Ellipse (Without rotation) : \( \delta = \pm \frac{\pi}{2} \) $$ \frac{x^2}{A^2} + \frac{y^2}{B^2} = 1 $$

(4)Other ellipse: \( \delta = \) other angle

Anisotropic Harmonic Oscillator

The motion of the kind of oscillators is Lissajous Curve It is the combination of two sine waves with different ratios between phases and amplitudes.

$$ \begin{cases} x(\theta) = a\sin(\theta) \\ y(\theta) = b\sin(n\theta + \phi) \end{cases} $$

In this chapter, we rewrite the definition of a Lissajous curve into

$$ \left\{ \begin{array}{l} x(t) = A\cos(\omega_x t) \\ y(t) = B\cos(\omega_y t - \delta) \end{array} \right. $$
